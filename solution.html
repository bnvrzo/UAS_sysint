Tower Construction

This service covers the complete end-to-end process of building new telecommunication towers from scratch. It includes land/site acquisition (negotiating with landowners or local governments), feasibility studies, obtaining all required permits (IMB/PBG, environmental permits, tower erection permits, etc.), geotechnical soil investigation, structural design & engineering (using software such as TNX Tower, MS Tower, or STAAD.Pro), foundation construction (pile, raft, or pad & chimney), tower fabrication, galvanizing, transportation, erection/crane lifting, installation of antennas, microwave dishes, RRUs, aviation obstruction lights, grounding & lightning protection systems, and final commissioning/handover. The service is provided for both greenfield sites (open land, often in rural or suburban areas) and rooftop sites (on top of buildings in dense urban areas). Most companies also offer Build-to-Suit (BTS) models where the tower company builds the tower according to the exact specifications and timeline requested by a specific mobile operator.

Maintenance Services

Comprehensive preventive and corrective maintenance to ensure 99.9%+ uptime of the tower and its associated equipment. This includes 24/7 monitoring (via NOC/remote sensors), scheduled site visits (monthly/quarterly), diesel generator refueling & maintenance, battery replacement, air-conditioner servicing for shelters, tower painting & anti-climb systems, structural inspections (visual, bolt torque checks, plumb & twist measurement), rust treatment, tightening of loose members, replacement of damaged parts, aviation light repairs, vegetation control around the site, security guarding, power management (hybrid solar-diesel solutions), alarm systems, and emergency rapid-response teams in case of outages or natural disasters. Many companies now offer predictive maintenance using IoT sensors and AI analytics to predict failures before they happen.

Leasing and Colocation

The core revenue model for most independent towercos. The tower owner rents out available slots (alpha, beta, gamma sectors) on the tower to multiple mobile network operators (MNOs) or other tenants (broadband providers, government radio systems, private LTE networks, etc.). Each colocation package typically includes antenna mounting space, outdoor or indoor equipment space (shelter/cabinet), feeder cable routing, DC/AC power supply, backup batteries/generator, fiber optic or microwave connectivity to the core network, and ongoing maintenance. Contracts are usually long-term (5–15 years) with built-in escalation clauses and amendment options for future technology upgrades (e.g., adding 5G massive MIMO antennas). This shared infrastructure model dramatically reduces capital expenditure and speeds up network rollout for operators.

Design and Consulting Services

Highly specialized structural and civil engineering services that include:
– Tower load capacity analysis and rigging plans
– Structural modification designs when adding new tenants or upgrading to heavier 5G equipment
– Foundation reinforcement and soil stabilization
– Wind, seismic analysis according to TIA-222-H or local standards
– 3D modeling and clash detection
– Permit drawings and as-built documentation
– Regulatory compliance consulting (SNI 1726, SNI 1727 in Indonesia, FAA/ICAO aviation marking in other countries)
– Due-diligence and valuation reports for tower portfolio acquisitions
Many towercos have in-house licensed professional engineers (PE) or partner with certified engineering firms to provide these services either standalone or bundled with construction/maintenance contracts.
