PT Towerindo Secures Rp 4.2 Trillion Funding to Accelerate 5G Rollout Across Eastern Indonesia

Jakarta, 7 December 2025 – PT Towerindo Tbk (TOWR2), one of Indonesia’s fastest-growing independent tower companies, today announced the successful closing of a Rp 4.2 trillion syndicated loan and green bond facility led by Bank Mandiri, BNI, and international lenders including IFC and DEG. The proceeds will be used to construct 3,500 new towers and upgrade 4,000 existing sites with 5G-ready infrastructure, with a strong focus on Eastern Indonesia, Kalimantan, and Sulawesi.
Since its establishment in 2016, PT Towerindo has rapidly expanded its portfolio to over 12,000 towers and 18,000 tenancy contracts, achieving an impressive average tenancy ratio of 1.52x. The company recently completed the acquisition of 1,800 towers from a regional operator in Sumatra and Sulawesi, further solidifying its position as the country’s third-largest towercos behind Mitratel and Protelindo.

“We are not just building towers; we are bridging the digital divide,” said Andreas Wijaya, President Director of PT Towerindo. “This funding allows us to bring high-speed mobile broadband to underserved provinces and support the government’s vision of 100% 4G/5G coverage by 2028.”
The company also highlighted its commitment to sustainability: 40% of the new sites will be equipped with hybrid solar-diesel power systems, and all new towers will comply with the latest SNI seismic and wind-load standards.
Shares of PT Towerindo rose 6.8% in morning trading on the Indonesia Stock Exchange following the announcement. Analysts expect the company to reach 20,000 towers by the end of 2027, positioning it as a strong contender in Indonesia’s consolidating tower infrastructure market.

PT Towerindo Wins “Best Tower Company of the Year 2025” and Announces 2,000 New Rural Towers

Bali, 7 December 2025 – PT Towerindo Tbk has been named “Telecom Infrastructure Company of the Year 2025” at the prestigious Selular Selular Award 2025 ceremony held last night in Nusa Dua, Bali. The award recognizes the company’s outstanding contribution to digital inclusion, operational excellence, and fastest network expansion pace among independent tower companies in Indonesia over the past 12 months.
In his acceptance speech, CEO Maya Santoso revealed an ambitious new target: deploying 2,000 additional greenfield towers in rural and remote areas by the end of 2026, primarily in Papua, Maluku, NTT, and frontier islands. “These are not ordinary towers — every site will come with fiber backhaul or high-capacity microwave, solar-hybrid power, and full 5G capability from day one,” she stated.
The announcement comes just weeks after PT Towerindo achieved a major milestone of 15,000 total tenancies and an industry-leading tenancy ratio of 1.68x. The company also launched its “Desa 5G” program in partnership with the Ministry of Communication and Informatics (Kominfo), aiming to bring high-speed internet to 500 unconnected villages within the next 18 months.
Market observers praise Towerindo’s aggressive yet disciplined growth strategy, strong balance sheet, and reputation for completing projects ahead of schedule. With this latest recognition and rural expansion plan, PT Towerindo further cements its position as Indonesia’s most dynamic and socially responsible tower infrastructure provider.

PT Towerindo Partners with Starlink to Deploy 1,000 Hybrid Satellite-Tower Sites in Remote Archipelagos

PT Towerindo Tbk (TOWR2) has signed a landmark strategic cooperation agreement with Starlink Indonesia to roll out 1,000 integrated satellite-tower sites over the next 24 months, targeting 3T regions (terdepan, terluar, tertinggal) that remain unreachable by terrestrial fiber or microwave.
Under the deal, Towerindo will construct low-impact, rapid-deploy monopole towers (20–30 meters) equipped with Starlink high-performance user terminals as primary backhaul, while simultaneously preparing sites for future fiber arrival. Each location will support 4G/5G small cells capable of serving up to 1 Gbps speeds for surrounding villages, schools, health centers, and local government offices.
“This is a game-changer for Indonesia’s outermost islands,” said Budi Hartono, Chief Technology Officer of PT Towerindo. “We can now deliver broadband within weeks instead of years, at a fraction of traditional backhaul solutions.”
The first 200 hybrid sites are scheduled to go live in Q2 2026 across Maluku, North Maluku, and Papua Barat Daya. The project is partially funded through Towerindo’s recently issued US$150 million sustainability-linked bond, with KPIs tied to the number of newly connected citizens.
Following the announcement, Towerindo shares surged 9.3% to a new all-time high of Rp 1,850 on the Indonesia Stock Exchange. Analysts now project the company to surpass 20,000 total sites by 2028, driven by both organic growth and innovative satellite integration.
